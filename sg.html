Sample Genius Mobile Application

Discover the Story, Uncover the Soul

Sample Genius solves the need for music enthusiasts to seamlessly explore the history, origins, and context behind their favorite songs while discovering new music aligned with their unique tastes.

My Role: UX/UI Designer

Team Members: 4

Time: 3 weeks

Tools Used: InVision, Figma, Adobe Illustrator, Milanote, Google Forms

Problem

As user researchers, we want to understand how users locate and find information about music that they are interested in.

Solution

During this project, we were assigned the task of designing a new mobile application for music enthusiasts. This app allows them to discover the history behind their favorite songs including original music and samples that have inspired them.

Design Process

Qualitative Research

While conducting this study, our group created a proto persona of what we thought the typical user would be like for our application. Then we created a list of 10 questions to conduct a short survey that we would send off on social media platforms. We, also created a research script of 20 questions that were relevant in discovering how users locate and discover new information about some of their favorite songs.

With these questions, we each interviewed 2-3 people between the ages of 20 to 60 years of age who listen to a variety of music. These people have a large interest in and knowledge of music. These people also have an interest in movies, tv shows, social media, or other pop culture platforms since that would typically be where users might have recognized the songs from. After analyzing the research gathered, we created an affinity diagram and an empathy map to figure out the true user persona.

“Music has a great way of influencing your mood. ”
“I have a lot of curiosity…you look up the songwriter, and you find some other things…you find interesting things.”
“I like to know like samples within songs…I like to know what inspired that song.”
“I think a lot of people and myself can relate to a lot of songs and music.”

Survey Information

We used Google Forms to create a 10 question survey. This survey was used to obtain quantitative data. We posted the survey to social media sites and also sent out the survey to anyone we thought would be interested in music or music information. The questions revolved around how often users listened to music, what applications they use to listen it, and what kind of information music enthusiasts are interested in learning about songs they listen to.

User Persona

User Insight

Ryan, a music enthusiast that is passionate about learning, needs a dynamic and educational music application that provides history, origins and context behind some of his favorite songs because he wants to nurture his passion for music discovery, have a better understanding of his connection with the music and share that information with others.

Problem Statement

How might we improve Sample Genius so that music enthusiasts are successful based on an increase in user retention, in song context views, and in sharing their findings on social media platforms.

User Scenario

Ryan is watching a movie and hears a song that strikes him. He wants to know what the song is and thinks he has heard it somewhere else before. He needs a way to identify the song and find out where he has heard it before.

Goals

Quickly identify songs that he has heard

Dive deeper into the background information such as where the song has been featured

Consolidate his music research tools into a single application

Experience

Opportunities

Identify songs based off vague verbal description provided by the user. Have the app run in the background to recognize all songs playing without having to ask. Create daily playlist of songs the user head in their environment as they went about their day. Provide information about identified artist's upcoming events, concerts, and updates. Provide verified user input about songs and their backgrounds.
